Tournament Details

Entry Fee

An entry fee of $10 will be collected at the course prior to our tee time. Please be sure to be there to pay at least 15 minutes before tee time. Our glorious staff needs a little time to hit a few balls or at least a few putts.

The $10 Prize money is allocated as follows:

  • $5 for Low Net
    A minimum of 1/3 of the field gets paid. If there are 15 players in a flight, then 5 players get paid. If there are 16 players, then 6 get paid.
  • $3 for Gross Skins
  • $2 for Closest to Pin

Odd dollars and cents are put into the Hole in One fund. For example, if there are 16 players in a flight (that's $48 for gross skins) and if there are 5 skins, the payout per skin will be $48/5 = $9 plus $3 left over which will go to the Hole in One pot.

Your winnings will be held for you until the next time you play and sign in. It will be held for 60 days at which time it will go into the Hole in One pot. If you know you will be gone for 60+ days, notify Tom Moore and he will see that it is held until you return.

When do we play?

Tournaments are played every Monday and Wednesday throughout the year except when it conflicts with a holiday or bad weather. On Mondays we usually play Waterchase and Wednesday we play any one of a dozen or more other courses in the area. Tee times are early in the morning depending on the time of year - typically 7:30 in the summer, 8:00 in the early spring and late fall, and 8:30 in the winter. Tee times may change based on the weather conditions or by request of the golf course.

What do we play?

Based on your handicap, you will be slotted into a flight with players that have similar handicaps. Flights are typically 12-16 players although this may vary depending on the number of players signed up. You will usually play in a threesome or foursome with players in the same flight, although this is not always the case. You may ask to play with friends in the same or a different flight.

All play is individual within your flight

with prize money awarded in four categories.
  • Low Net: Your gross score for 18 holes minus your handicap
  • Gross Skins: he single best gross score for each hole
  • CTP: Closest to the Pin on all Par 3s
  • Low Gross: Optionally, for an additional $5, you can also play for Low Gross within your flight.

Pace of play

It is our goal to play 18 holes in no more than 4h 15m. Besides this being a very reasonable time to play in, the course will have scheduled tee times right behind us. If we are slow, then those tee times will not be able to start until our last group has passed the 1st tee. So if we are too slow getting past the 1st tee too often, we may not be welcome back.

Exceptions to USGA Rules of Golf

When possible, we try to play by the USGA Rules of Golf. However, as senior golfers, and because we want to finish within the 4h 15m goal, some exceptions to those rules have been made. There is always some confusion about what those exceptions are so please follow this link to the Grumpy Old Men Signup. Unless otherwise notified, the deadline for signing up is 5:00 PM the day before the tournament. Most tournaments have a maximum number of players as set by the golf course. If it is already full when you try to sign up, you can (if you want) be put on a waiting list in case there is a cancellation.

  • Signing IN

    • For each tournament you Sign UP for, you will Sign IN at the golf course by:

    • Paying your green fee in the Pro Shop (usually between $40 and $45). The green fee includes a cart and range balls, although some courses charge extra for range balls.
    • Paying an entry fee of $10 at the Sign-in Desk (manned by some really Grumpy Old Men) where they collect your $10 and tell you who you are playing with and what hole you start on (this is a shotgun start). The $10 goes into a prize pot.

    You must be at the course at least 15 minutes prior to the tee time to sign in. The sign-in desk will close at that time. The people at this desk are also playing in the tournament and need this time to finish up and maybe hit a few putts.

    Rules of Golf

    We play by the USGA Rules of Golf but, considering our age, trying to make the game more fun, and to improve the speed of play, there are a few exceptions to those rules. For example, some flights may play preferred lies in the fairway or there is a maximum of double par on any hole. These rule exceptions are explained in detail in Rule Exceptions

    Welcome to Potential New Members

    This is a high level overview of the Grumpy Old Men (aka The Grumpies) senior golf group. If you decide to join, or have already joined, we welcome you. If you decide not to join, we hope you change your mind.

    Membership

    All golfers age 55 or older are invited to join us every Monday and Wednesday throughout the year. A one-time membership fee of $10 will secure your membership. Just send an email to Join Grumpy Old Men Golf. Include your name, age, handicap, email address, and phone number. You can pay the membership fee the first time you play in one of our tournaments.
